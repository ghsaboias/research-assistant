3. Analysis of Main Points

Incumbent Mayor Ricardo Nunes

Ricardo Nunes of the MDB party took over as mayor in 2021 after the death of Bruno Covas. As the incumbent, Nunes is widely expected to be a leading contender in the 2024 election. His campaign platform emphasizes a focus on economic stability, infrastructure improvements, and maintaining the status quo in Sao Paulo's governance. Nunes has secured the support of several center-right and center-left parties, including the PSDB-Cidadania alliance, forming a diverse coalition.

Challenger Guilherme Boulos

Boulos, a federal deputy and leader of the Homeless Workers' Movement, was the runner-up in the 2020 mayoral election. He has the backing of President Lula and the PT party, which has caused some internal division within the PT in Sao Paulo. Boulos is positioning himself as a progressive, left-wing alternative focused on addressing the city's social inequalities, improving public services, and implementing more ambitious economic and environmental policies. While the PT leadership has officially endorsed Boulos, some factions within the party have expressed resistance, fearing that his alliance with the PSOL may alienate more centrist voters.

Other Pre-Candidates

The field also includes veteran journalist José Luiz Datena running for the PSDB-Cidadania alliance, federal deputy Tabata Amaral of the PSB, and economist Marina Helena of the NOVO party. These candidates represent a range of political ideologies and backgrounds, with Datena appealing to more conservative voters, Amaral targeting centrist and progressive supporters, and Helena offering a market-oriented platform.

Election Calendar and Potential Runoff

The election is scheduled for October 6th, 2024, with a potential second round runoff on October 27th if no candidate wins a majority in the first round. This setup could lead to an intense campaign season and runoff battle between the top two finishers, likely Nunes and Boulos based on current polling data.
